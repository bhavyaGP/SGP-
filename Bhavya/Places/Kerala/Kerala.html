About

Kerala, fondly known as "God's Own Country," is a mesmerizing destination that boasts an enchanting tapestry of backwaters, lush green landscapes, and serene beaches. This coastal paradise, located in southern India, is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the winter months from October to March. The weather is pleasant, and the cool breeze adds to the charm of exploring the picturesque alleys of Fort Kochi and cruising along the tranquil backwaters of Alleppey in a traditional houseboat. Kerala's rich cultural heritage comes to life during the vibrant festivals, such as Onam, where elaborate flower carpets and traditional dance performances showcase the region's diversity. Savor the delectable cuisine, especially the flavorsome seafood delicacies, and pamper yourself with Ayurvedic treatments at one of the many wellness retreats. Whether you seek a tranquil retreat or an adventure in the lush Western Ghats, Kerala offers an unforgettable journey that will leave you yearning for more of its idyllic beauty.

Best time to visit

The best time to visit Kerala is during the winter months, from October to February. This season offers pleasant weather with milder temperatures and low humidity, providing the perfect conditions to explore the state's diverse landscapes and cultural attractions. Enjoy leisurely boat cruises through the iconic backwaters, witness the picturesque green hills of Munnar, and bask in the serenity of Kerala's pristine beaches. The famous Nehru Trophy Boat Race and various cultural festivals held during this time offer a glimpse into the rich heritage and vibrant traditions of the region. If you prefer to witness Kerala's lush greenery and experience Ayurvedic treatments, the monsoon season from June to September can be equally enchanting. However, the winter months promise the most delightful and comfortable experience, making it the ideal time to immerse yourself in the beauty and allure of "God's Own Country."
